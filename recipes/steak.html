How to make Steak

Description:

This recipe involves a steak being cooked in the reverse-sear method. This method is not for those who want a quick steak but for those who want to retain as much juices as possible in the steak. Easy to follow recipe!

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 inch to 2 inch steak (I prefer Ribeye or New York Strip)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Garlic Powder
  • High-smoke point oil
  • Patience

Equipment:

  • Oven
  • Heavy pan, cast iron or stainless steel skillet works best
  • Stove
  • Tongs
  • Baking sheet with wire rack
  • Thermometer

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 275° F
  2. Pat dry steak with paper towels
  3. Generously season steak with seasonings on all sides
  4. Let steak sit for at least 40 minutes at room temperature.
    5. (Optional: Let steak brine overnight uncovered in fridge for maximum flavor.)
  5. Place steak on baking sheet with wire rack and then place in oven
  6. Check on the steak's temperature in 15 minute intervals until the temperature in the center reaches 10-15° F under your desired doneness.
    7. (I take it out at 115° F for Medium Rare)
  7. Heat up pan on high heat and add oil once smoking
  8. Take baking sheet out of oven and immediately place steak in pan.
  9. Take steak out once crust is developed on both sides and ideal internal temperature is reached
  10. No need to rest your steak if using this method so cut into your steak and enjoy!
